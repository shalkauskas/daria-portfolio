Donation feature for Venmo app (conceptual)

PROJECT OVERVIEW

Category:
UX, Mobile Design


Timeline: 2 weeks sprint


# of Team members: 4


My role:
Part of research, sketching, wireframing and prototyping the user flow that describes how the user makes a donation while sending money to his friend.


Tools:
Sketch, InVision

Results

The ability to donate a dollar to the cause of your choice while completing regular Venmo transactions was integrated to the existing app. The social aspect, that makes Venmo unique and distinctive, is enhanced by showing donations made by others in the user feed. User got the feeling of being secure dealing with charity as he is the one who makes a choice of the organization to support and decide whether or not it needs to be public.

Research

Interviews

Key takeaways from interviews:

  • Everyone wants the donation process to be quick and easy.
  • Users are more likely to donate when they are offered to do so.
  • Venmo is mostly used for small amount transactions, often to split the charge among a group of people.
  • When you donate it is important to know where your money is going.
  • People enjoy friends and family
  • Anonymity should always be an option

Problem statement:

The Venmo Superuser (and the Venmo Do-gooder) need a fast and easy way to donate to charities that resonate with them.

Solution:

We believe that by adding a donation feature for Venmo Superusers, we will enable them to donate more easily. We will know this to be true when we see 20% of Venmo users donate at least once a month.

The “1$” concept

  • One dollar is the amount that wouldn’t hurt anyone’s budget.
  • When you are asked for just a dollar you don’t feel that much pressure.>
  • According to the interview answers, people, who do not tend to donate consciously, still make small donations when they are asked to do so, For example, at the cashier while doing the grocery shopping.
  • One dollar means a lot for charity. Currently, there over 20 million users of the Venmo app. Just imagine: if at least 20% of Venmo users donate a dollar the amount will be $4 million! Pretty impressive. huh?

Let Venmo stay Venmo!

During our first team brainstorming, we had a bunch of discussions on where user can find the donation feature. First, we tried to switch it with the global news feed as most of our research participants stated that they don’t get its role at the Venmo app. But then we realized that our goal is to add a new feature and NOT CHANGE THE APP.

The social feed is “the secret sauce” that makes Venmo unique and distinguish it among other money-sharing apps. So we ended up adding the button “donate to a charity” to the sidebar menu leaving the home page of the app untouched.

Sketches

Button VS Checkbox

Issue

When I sketched my userflow I was hesitating if I should make "donate $1" as a button that changes the color/opacity when you click on it or a checkbox.

My actions

In order to figure out what works better I did some usability tests. The feedback I got shown that when users see the button, they expect it to send them to another page (which makes it unnecessary complicated). The checkbox indicates that the action can be made with 1 click.

Iterations

  • At our first wireframes, we tried to make the charity organization logo stand out, so we had it in a square. During some usability tests (I personally conducted 4 of them), we received feedback that it looks weird and doesn’t blend to Venmo's interface. So we switched to the popular round shape.
  • The common question from users was: "What if I want to donate more than a dollar?". We had lots of discussions at our team regarding this issue. We tried to switch to "Donate" instead, but then we got an insight that users want to have an easy path as well. We ended up with keeping both ways to please both our personas.

Project takeaways:

  • Good communication is an integral part of a successful team.
  • It is important to have short-cuts and several approaches to have things done.
  • If you do not know what works better, go test!
